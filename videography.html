Shan & Videography

I’ve always been the “recording friend” - the one with their phone out
capturing the funny, chaotic, and fleeting moments. I started editing around
age 10, running a small YouTube channel with my friends and learning how
to turn everyday moments into stories.

Over the last decade, that habit has grown into a steady creative practice.
Documenting real moments and shaping them into engaging content is something
I genuinely love, and it’s a big part of what led me to GBDA.

Below is a selection of some of my videography work I’m proud of.

ACS Documentary - GBDA 201

For our final project in GBDA 201, we were asked to form groups of four and work collaboratively to create a 4–8 minute documentary on a phenomenon our classmates “had never seen before”.

Our piece explores how uWaterloo students of Caribbean descent stay connected to home through the resident Association of Caribbean Students (ACS). More specifically, it focuses on Caribbean party culture and ACS’s upcoming fete, "Balance".

This story is especially meaningful to me as a student from Barbados and a member of the ACS executive board.

CIF Silent Film - GBDA 201

For our second project in GBDA 201, we were tasked with creating a one-minute cinematic portrait of a place, using documentary footage to give the audience a genuine sense of “being there.” The film had to be completely silent, incorporate a range of distinct shot types, and convey both spatial awareness and the passage of time.

I chose to focus on the Columbia Ice Field (CIF), one of my favourite buildings on campus. I’m there often, and it felt fitting to spotlight a space many students rarely visit or fully understand.

GBDA 101 A3 - Emotional Design

For Assignment 3 in GBDA 101, we were each given an emotion at random and tasked with transforming an everyday object into something that conveyed that feeling. We then had to create a two minute short film that showcased our reimagined object in action.

My assigned emotion was “adventurous,” and so I transformed a baking pan into a board game designed to encourage students to explore Waterloo with their friends.
